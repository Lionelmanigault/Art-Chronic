Featured Contemporary Artist: Yong Jo Ji

Yong Jo Ji's work is a layered fusion of deconstructed art techniques, media and materials reconstituted and integrated in a modern day context. Disintegrated pop-culture images from advertisements and recycled industrial materials awaken new perceptions and meanings of the public world; layers of recurring and constantly evolving forms and images overlap and sync together allowing each mark its own importance and recognition; underneath the rhythm of the work there is foundation in the depth of the human sub consciousness. The rhythms of repetition and change are seated deeply into the human sub consciousness through the rhythms in nature – the cycles of day and night, the seasons, the reproduction of plants and animals. His awareness, His dialogue and interaction with the environment, becomes the work itself – it is our collective energy and expression; a visual language that could be understood by all, connecting the universal and the individual organically. Art Chronic's Mission

Art Chronic has partnered with Soho Arts Club to raise awareness of three causes: Effectively preventing ill health, hospitalization and unnecessary death with Health People; giving equal opportunity for all to advance in life through programming with NYCDA, while reviving the Downtown creative scene with Soho Arts Club and Yong Jo Ji.

On June 6, 2015 at the SoHo Art Club venue, where the likes of Andy Warhol has produces his work and celebrities like ASAP Rocky have performed, a public art opening with food and vodka that is sponsored by Ychilli, will be held introducing Defy Ventures, Nobel Peace Prize Nominee Chris Norwood who is the founder and executive director of Health People. This celebration will be accompanied by an exhibition of artist Yong Jo Ji’s most exquisite paintings.

On June 9, 2015 the SoHo Art Club will be hosting the public opening of Yong Jo Ji: An Exhibition of Synchronicity. The opening finale will be held on June 11, 2015 that includes a performances by Alek Sanders (Visit www.alek-sandar.com.) . Public viewing will be from 6:00 PM to 9:00 PM on both evenings.
